About Me

Built like it was engineered.
Because it was.

Most software problems are really systems problems. I'm a registered Engineering Technologist (ECSA) — trained to find where things fail before they do. I came to software through a genuine interest in systems and what makes them break.

The ECSA registration and a Postgraduate Diploma in Business Administration from UFS are just evidence that I take both sides of a problem seriously — the technical and the human cost of getting it wrong.

The cycle

Saved time reduces errors. Fewer errors mean fewer comebacks. Fewer comebacks free up focus. Focus produces quality. Quality reduces stress. Reduced stress sustains performance. The loop runs itself.

改善 — Kaizen
改善 (Kaizen) — Japanese: "change for better"

The philosophy of continuous improvement through small, consistent gains. Not a single breakthrough — a system that compounds. Originally from Japanese manufacturing, now a core principle in business and software.
